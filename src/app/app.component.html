Dean's Student Showcase

October 30th, 2018
Engineering VI, 7-9pm

The Largest Collection of Talent at UCLA

More than your average career fair, the Dean’s Student Showcase is a partnership between the Samueli School of Engineering and UCLA’s engineering honor societies to put on exclusive, one-of-a-kind recruiting event. With admission limited to only honor society members, this the perfect opportunity to interact with some of the best candidates UCLA has to offer.

Meet the Honor Societies

Tau Beta Pi | TBP

Tau Beta Pi is the engineering honor society at UCLA, representing all engineering disciplines. Members have distinguished themselves academically by climbing to the top eighth of the junior class or top fifth of the senior class and have shown exemplary character through service to the community.

Eta Kappa Nu | HKN

Eta Kappa Nu (HKN) is an Electrical and Computer Engineering honor society that recognizes students who are in the top fourth of the junior class or top third of the senior class. Our members have demonstrated their dedication by serving fellow members of the Electrical and Computer Engineering community.

Chi Epsilon | XE

Chi Epsilon is a civil engineering honor society where students and professionals are selected to become members based on recognition of their scholarship, character, practicality and sociability. For student members, scholarship is determined by being in the top third of their junior or senior class. Members of Chi Epsilon are considered top graduates and are highly sought by civil engineering employers.

Upsilon Pi Epsilon | UPE

Upsilon Pi Epsilon is an international honor society created to recognize academic excellence in the Computing and Information Disciplines. The members of our UCLA chapter are among the most outstanding undergraduate Computer Science students at UCLA. Our chapter holds events for students aiming to help them gain the skills and information that will help grow both professionally and academically.
